About

Welcome to the homepage for the Directed Reading program of the Statistics and Probability Association at the University of Washington.

What is a DRP?

A Directed Reading Program is a one-on-one mentoring program in which graduate students mentor undergrads in reading projects. Initiated by graduate students in mathematics at the Universiy of Chicago in 2003, DRPs now run in mathematics departments at 17 institutions around the country. DRPs foster community between graduate students and undergraduates, help undergraduates develop vital academic skills and engage with material outside of the classroom, and provide graduate students with valuable mentoring experience. For more information, see the webpage of the DRP Network .

What is SPA?

SPA is the undergraduate Statistics and Probability Association at the University of Washington.

What is the SPA DRP?

Our program will run for the first time during Winter Quarter, 2020. We invite students from all mathematical, statistical, and personal backgrounds to apply. The main function of the directed reading program is to introduce undergraduate students to statistics outside of the classroom environment and help them gain skills in critically reading statistical texts and communicating statistical material. These skills can be gained in various ways, and there will be projects catered to various levels of statistical experience. For more information about the program goals, see the about page.
